I build the infrastructure that makes AI systems provably trustworthy. Not promising. Not probably. Provably — with a ledger, a seal, and a methodology that cannot be delegated back to the AI.

Most AI reliability work is reactive. Something fails, then someone writes a post-mortem. The failure disappears into a Slack thread and the next failure surprises everyone again.

The AION Constitutional Stack is the opposite. It was built through live failure events — not theoretical modeling. Every framework in the stack exists because something broke in a specific way that a prior framework couldn't catch. The ledger is the record. The seal is the proof. The methodology is falsifiable.

Operating Principles

  • Structural Honesty First. Map the validated ceiling before enabling acceleration. M-NASCENT means specified, not proven. The label matters.

  • Falsification Before Claim. Certainty engineering means knowing exactly where confidence is justified — and naming the boundary, not smoothing over it.

  • Failure Is Data. An organization with a high failure rate and a complete ledger is more trustworthy than one with no failures on record and no ledger. The ledger with failures is honest.

  • The Verification Layer Cannot Be Delegated. You cannot ask an AI whether its own output is trustworthy. Human oversight is not a limitation of the current systems. It is the correct architecture.

  • Law 9 Is Dark. The sovereignty stack declares its own boundary. Premature specification of what comes next would be confabulation.
